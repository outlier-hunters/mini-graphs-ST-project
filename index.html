What is Cryptocurrency?

Cryptocurrency is a digital currency that uses cryptographic technology for secure transactions. Unlike traditional money, cryptocurrencies operate on decentralized networks, typically based on blockchain technology.

How Do Crypto Markets Work?

Crypto markets function 24/7, allowing users to trade digital assets based on supply and demand. The price of a cryptocurrency fluctuates based on various factors, including trading volume, market trends, and global events.

Key Market Metrics

  • Market Cap
    Market Capitalization (Market Cap) represents the total value of a cryptocurrency, calculated as price × circulating supply.
  • 24h Trading Volume
    Trading Volume shows the total amount of a cryptocurrency traded in the last 24 hours, indicating market activity.
  • Candlestick Charts
    Candlestick Charts help visualize price movements, showing opening, closing, high, and low prices in a given period.

What is a Candlestick Chart?

A candlestick chart represents the price movements of an asset over a specific period. Each "candlestick" shows four key price points:

  • Open Price – The price at which the asset started during that time period.
  • Close Price – The price at which the asset ended during that time period.
  • High Price – The highest price reached during that period.
  • Low Price – The lowest price reached during that period.

How to Read It?

  • Green (or White) Candles → The asset’s price went up (closed higher than it opened).
  • Red (or Black) Candles → The asset’s price went down (closed lower than it opened).
  • The thin lines (wicks or shadows) show the highest and lowest prices.

Some terms:

  • Bullish meaning went up in simple terms, means optimistic. This usually refers to the belief that a specific investment or market index will rise, but it can also be used more broadly to mean "good times ahead." Someone might say they're "bullish on the United States" to indicate that they think the U.S. economy will be strong in the years ahead.
  • Bearish meaningwent down Conversely, bearish basically means pessimistic. The term usually means someone thinks the price of an investment will go down, but it has a more general usage as well. Someone might say they're bearish on an entire industry, such as, for example, fast food, meaning that they think that industry will fall out of favor in the future.

Understanding the Multi-Axis Line Chart

A Multi-Axis Line Chart tracks the price movements of multiple cryptocurrencies simultaneously. Each cryptocurrency has its own scale, allowing for accurate comparison despite different price ranges.

How to Read This Chart?
